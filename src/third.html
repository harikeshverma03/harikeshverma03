Third Generation (1964-1971):Integrated Circuits

  • Users interacted with third-generations computers through keyboards and monitors.
  • For the first time, computers became accessible to a mass audience because they were smaller and cheaper than their predecessors
  • The development of the integrated circuit was the hallmark of the third-generation computers.
  • Transistors were miniaturised and placed on silicon chips, called semiconductors, which drastically increased the speed and efficiency of computers.

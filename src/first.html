First Generation (1940-1956):Vaccum Tubes

  • The first generation computers used vaccum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory.They were often enormous, taking up entire rooms
  • First generation computers relied on machine language.
  • They were expensive to operate, in addition to using a great deal of electricity, generated a lot of heat,which was often the case of malfuntions
  • UNIVAC and ENIAC were examples of first-generation computing devices
